2.a

Here we are refitting the affairs data into a normal poisson model to display how poisson is flawed in capturing extreme overdispersion within out data. We will use the affairs data again.

mod_pois = glm(n_affairs ~ marriage_rating + age + kids + years_married, 
               data = new_affairs, family = poisson)

# Fitted Values and Residuals 
mu_hat = fitted(mod_pois)

pearson_terms = (new_affairs$n_affairs - mu_hat)^2 / mu_hat
pearson_sum = sum(pearson_terms)

# Get n and k to divide by residuals

n = nobs(mod_pois)

k = length(coef(mod_pois))

phi = pearson_sum / (n-k)
phi

2.b

Poisson assumes that our conditional variance is similar or congruent to our conditional mean, despite the presence concentrated tails and zeros. This phi value of about 7.22 is much much larger than 1, which is indicative of how badly the Poisson model fails to capture how spread out our data is.

3.a

leaders = leader |>
  group_by(country, leader) |>
  summarise(
    start_year = min(year),
    end_year   = max(year),
    duration   = max(yr_office),  # true spell length (1840–2015 view)
    
    rebel      = first(rebel, order_by = year),

  drop_na(duration, rebel) |>

3.d

The bias is coming from the right tail of the duration distribution — the very long-serving leaders whose tenures are still ongoing at the 1991 cutoff. In the Oracle histogram, you can see a small number of leaders with very long spells. Once you move to the “Observed” and the “Drop Censored” views, that upper tail gets compressed or removed.

OLS assumes outcomes are fully observed but censored observations are only partially observed. We know that leaders lasted at least til a certain time, not exactly how long. Survival-analysis machinery accounts for this discrepancy.

4.c

The signs of the fin variable are positive on both exponentiated models but not on our Cox model that measures in log hazards. So according to our exponential and weibull model, the presence of financial assistance after release implies a longer likelihood of staying out as the weeks go on. Our negative Cox coefficient implies a similar idea just in different terms. There is about a 36% percent lower hazard of being re-arrested when given financial assistance.
